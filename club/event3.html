Report on “HOVER CONNECTS”

Organized by Drone Club & IoT Club

Event Details

Date: 20th - 21st December 2024

Time: 09:00 AM - 4:00 PM

Venue: 20th – H block 215   |   21st – A block 217

Registrations: 23   |   Participants: 79

Objective

  • Main aim is to make participants understand the significance of IoT and Drone in various industries and real-world scenarios.
  • Introduce students to applications of sensors and microcontrollers in data collection and decision-making.
  • Teach methods and protocols to collect accurate, real-time sensor data and ensure efficient transmission.
  • Introduce basics of IoT technology and demonstrate integration with drone systems.

Day 1 Sessions

Session 1

Speakers: E. Kishan Karthik, J. Sirivarshini, Maniram, L. Hari Preetam

Topics covered: Arduino basics, LED/buzzer/distance sensor connections, IoT & drone components overview.

Session 2

Speakers: L. Hari Preetam, Maniram

Activities: Arduino IDE setup, LED & buzzer coding, hands-on practice with mini projects.

Day 2 Sessions

Session 1

Speaker: B. Mohnish

Topics covered: Tinkercad simulations (buzzers, ultrasonic sensors, RGB LEDs, soil moisture & PIR sensors).

Session 2

Activities: Hands-on with temperature sensors (DHT11/LM35), Arduino interfacing, data monitoring & practical applications.

Conclusion

The IoT X Drone workshop was an enriching experience that helped participants gain practical insights into IoT and drone systems. Thanks to the support of faculty coordinators and deans, the sessions provided valuable knowledge and hands-on experience. Collaboration between the IoT Club and Drone Club ensured participants had the resources to explore IoT + Drone applications for real-world solutions.
